The carbon footprint of clothes measures the emission of greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide, throughout its life cycle from raw material production to its end-of-life disposal. The key contributors to these emissions include energy consumption, fossil fuel use, and the production of the materials such as fibers or dyes. During manufacturing, the use of chemicals and heat further adds to greenhouse gas emissions. Additionally, transportation involves moving the raw materials, components and finished products usually over long-distance travel resulting in huge emissions. Even after purchase, consumers contribute to emissions through the electricity used for washing, drying or ironing clothes.
